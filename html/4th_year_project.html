4th Year Project – Odoo Helpdesk for TUD Health

Designing and configuring a hospital IT helpdesk using Odoo

Project Overview

For my 4th year project I worked with Odoo Helpdesk to d esign a support solution for a fictional healthcare organisation called TUD Health. The goal was to configure a cloud based helpdesk where hospital staff could log IT issues, track ti ckets and monitor service performance using dashboards and KPIs.

I set up a dedicated TUD Health database in Odoo, created helpdesk teams and configured ticket stages, communication channels and reporting views. The final system supports two key areas:

  • Customer Care – general support for staff
  • Hospital Support Desk – IT and equipment related tic kets

Environment Setup

The project started with the creation of an Odoo online database for TUD Health. The instance could be locked when inactive and reactivated when needed, which is shown below.

Internal Communication

To support collaboration inside the organisation, I configured the Discuss app with multiple channels:

  • general – announcements for all employees
  • chill – informal messages
  • outside work hours – messages that should not be ans wered during working time
  • Administrators – internal channel for system adminis trators

These channels help separate urgent operational messages from social conv ersations and admin tasks.

Helpdesk Dashboards and KPIs

The Dashboards app was used to visualise performance and workload for the helpdesk. I configured a Helpdesk dashboard that shows:

  • Total number of tickets in the last period
  • Average time to assign, respond and close tickets
  • Ticket distribution by stage (New, Solved, Waiting for User, Under In vestigation)
  • Tickets by team (Customer Care vs Hospital Support Desk)
  • Top customers and top assignees

These KPIs help managers quickly understand the volume and status of supp ort requests.

Helpdesk Overview

Within the Helpdesk module I configured teams, SLA targe ts and ticket categories. The overview page shows my own performance as an agent, inclu ding:

  • Number of open tickets assigned to me
  • High priority or urgent tickets
  • Average open hours
  • SLA success rate across the helpdesk

Working as an Agent

I created and worked on several example tickets to simulate real hospital IT issues such as: laptop not starting up and printer not working. The My Tickets view shows tickets assigned to me with filters for status and SLA deadlines.

Ticket Lifecycle and Reporting

I explored different ways for supervisors to monitor workload and progres s:

  • List view – all open tickets with priority, assignee , customer and stage
  • Kanban view – drag and drop tickets between stages s uch as New, Waiting for User and Under Investigation
  • Reporting graphs – bar charts of open tickets groupe d by team and stage

These views make it easy to see bottlenecks and balance tickets between a gents.
